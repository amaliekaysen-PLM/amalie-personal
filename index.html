Food Strategy

Most dinners are at refuges, hotels, or village restaurants. You only cook dinner at camp on Day 11 (Cheserys) — every other night has a hot-meal option. Breakfasts and snacks are always carried.

🎒 Always carry

Breakfast (instant oats, granola, coffee, powdered milk) for every morning · Trail snacks (nuts, dried fruit, chocolate, bars) · 2 L water capacity each · 1 emergency dinner ration.

🏠 Dinner at refuges/restaurants

12 of 13 trip nights have a hot meal option: Les Houches restaurant · Refuge de Miage · Refuge de la Balme · Auberge de la Nova (Chapieux) · Refugio Elisabetta (book ~2 weeks ahead) · Courmayeur restaurants · Camping GJ bar · Hôtel Edelweiss (La Fouly) · Café du Lac (Champex) · Refuge Le Peuty · Auberge la Boerne (Tré-le-Champ) · Refuge du Brévent.

🔥 Cook your own dinner

Just Day 11 (Cheserys bivouac). Carry one cooked meal (couscous, instant noodles, soup, or freeze-dried pouch) from your Champex resupply. Day 12 (Brévent) is also a bivouac but you can eat at Refuge du Brévent before pitching.

🛒 Resupply stops

Les Houches (Day 0) — Sherpa supermarket. Buy breakfasts + snacks for 2 days, plus gas canister (can't fly with one).
Les Contamines (Day 2) — Sherpa + bakery + fromagerie. Buy breakfasts + snacks for 3 days (next supermarket is Courmayeur on Day 5).
Courmayeur via Hobo bus (Day 5) — Carrefour Express + others. Buy breakfasts + snacks for 3 days (next supermarket is Champex on Day 8).
La Fouly (Day 7) — small village supermarket. Top up if needed.
Champex-Lac (Day 8) — supermarket + bakery. Last real supermarket of the trip. Buy breakfasts + snacks for 4–5 days AND 1 cooked dinner for the Cheserys bivouac (Day 11).
Argentière (Day 10 optional) — Sherpa. Final top-up before Aiguilles Rouges reserve.

Wild Camping & Bivouac Rules

🇫🇷 France

Bivouacking allowed between 7 PM and 9 AM. Tent must be pitched after 7 PM and taken down by 9 AM. Look for signed “Aire de Bivouac” areas. Fires are prohibited. Generally well-tolerated on the TMB if you are respectful and leave no trace.

⚠️ Aiguilles Rouges Nature Reserve (Days 11–12): Bivouac only in 6 designated zones. Booking mandatory via reserve-bivouac74.fr. Lac Blanc bivouac is prohibited.

🇮🇹 Italy (Aosta Valley)

Wild camping is officially prohibited below 2,500m — and the entire TMB Italy section is below this altitude. Fines can be issued (€100–500 pp).

Day 4 (Refugio Elisabetta, 2,195m): tolerated bivouac for refuge dinner guests if you pitch above the refuge, out of sight from the access road, after 7 PM, strike by 8 AM. Eating dinner at the refuge (~€20–25 pp) is the unwritten arrangement; refuge staff "don’t see you" if you’re discreet. Recent reports note tighter enforcement than older accounts.

Fully legal alternative: Camping Aiguille Noire at La Visaille (~1,500 m, ~€10 pp + €8/tent). Adds ~5–6 km to Day 4 but eliminates legal risk.

🇨🇭 Switzerland

Wild camping is prohibited. Bivouacking (single night, above treeline, small group) is tolerated in most areas but not in the Commune of Orsières (La Fouly area). Use official campsites.

Les Chapieux → Col de la Seigne → Pyramides Calcaires → Elisabetta 🇮🇹 Confirmed

📏Distance17.8
⬆Ascent935m
⬇Descent707m
⏱Time6–9h
Camping rule: Italy: wild camping officially prohibited below 2,500m. Elisabetta tolerates bivouacs in the meadows around the refuge for those buying a drink/meal — be discreet, pitch late, leave no trace.

🦶 Route

Climb steadily from Les Chapieux to Col de la Seigne (2,516m) — the France/Italy border. One of the great TMB moments: walk through the pass and the entire Italian side of Mont Blanc reveals itself, glaciers and all.

Pyramides Calcaires Punta Norte variant. Instead of dropping straight to Lac Combal, take the scenic ridge route that climbs UP from the col through the limestone pinnacles of the Pyramides Calcaires to ~2,577m. Wild, lunar terrain with the Lée Blanche glacier on your left and the whole Val Veny opening up below. The path drops you back onto the standard trail above Elisabetta.

Descend ~1 km on the main trail to the junction with Refugio Elisabetta Soldini (2,195m) — short detour up to the meadow above the refuge for the bivouac.

⛺ Where to Sleep

Bivouac in the meadows near Refugio Elisabetta Soldini (2,195m). Drinking water and toilet at the refuge; bar/restaurant for a hot drink before pitching. Spectacular setting at the head of Val Veny with the Lée Blanche glacier overhead. Pitch discreetly after 7 PM, well away from the refuge, leave no trace.

🎒 Carry
Lunch + tomorrow’s breakfast, snacks, water
🏠 Eat at refuge/restaurant
Dinner at Refugio Elisabetta
🛒 Resupply
None — next resupply is Courmayeur via bus from Hobo (Day 5)

🍽 Food & Resupply Details

  • Lunch: pack from Les Chapieux breakfast supplies, eat at Col de la Seigne or on the Pyramides Calcaires ridge
  • Dinner: eat at Refugio Elisabetta (~€20–25) — supports the bivouac arrangement and saves your stove fuel. Or cook at camp from your Les Contamines/Chapieux supplies.

💡 Tips

The Col de la Seigne crossing is magical in morning light — try to be on top by 11 AM. The Pyramides Calcaires variant is the scenic high-line; only attempt in clear weather (it's exposed and trail markings are sparser than the main TMB).

Elisabetta gets busy — pitch late (after 19:00) in the meadows uphill of the refuge, not on the trail. Eating dinner at the refuge (~€20–25) buys goodwill for the bivouac. Water from the refuge tap is potable.

Note on the map: the GPX polyline shows the Pyramides Calcaires high variant and ends near the Elisabetta trail junction — the actual bivouac is ~10 min up the spur to the meadows above the refuge.

Hobo → Mont de la Saxe → Bonatti → Camping GJ 🇮🇹 Confirmed

Tuesday, July 28 · Up to the Mont de la Saxe cairn (decide at Bertone), balcony to Bonatti, descend to Val Ferret
Side-trip: Mont de la Saxe cairn (~2,348m) — bail-to-balcony if weather/legs say no
📏Distance23.5
⬆Ascent1,600m
⬇Descent1,400m
⏱Time8½–10h
Camping rule: Italy: official campsite — no restrictions.

🦶 Route

From Hobo Camping, descend Val Veny to the valley floor (~1,170m), then climb the steady switchbacks up to Rifugio Bertone (1,989m) — a 2.5 h push from the river. Lunch on the terrace.

Decision point at Bertone. If the sky is clear and the legs feel good, take the signed Mont de la Saxe path UP the ridge from Bertone: roughly +350 m in 1.5 km of grassy switchbacks to the Mont de la Saxe cairn (~2,348m) — the Brenva face of Mont Blanc straight across the valley, Grandes Jorasses to the north. Drop back down the same way to rejoin the trail. If the weather is marginal or legs are toast, skip the detour and stay on the balcony — you lose nothing but the cairn view.

Continue along the Bertone–Bonatti balcony to Rifugio Bonatti (2,025m). Sunset on the terrace, then descend ~1 h down through the forest to Camping Grandes Jorasses in Lavachey (~1,600m).

⛺ Where to Sleep

Camping Grandes Jorasses, Lavachey (~1,600m) — tent pitch ~€12–18pp. Hot showers, bar/restaurant, small shop. Beautiful setting in Val Ferret beneath the Grandes Jorasses glacier.

🎒 Carry
Breakfast, snacks, water
🏠 Eat at refuge/restaurant
Lunch at Rifugio Bertone, dinner at Camping Grandes Jorasses bar/restaurant
🛒 Resupply
Small shop at campsite — you resupplied in Courmayeur yesterday

🍽 Food & Resupply Details

  • Breakfast: cook at Hobo before departing (early!)
  • Lunch: Rifugio Bertone — terrace with Grandes Jorasses views (~€12–18)
  • Dinner: cook at campsite or eat at the Grandes Jorasses bar/restaurant

💡 Tips

⚠️ Big day with the Saxe detour — start by 6:00 AM. Budget: Hobo→valley 1 h · valley→Bertone 2.5 h · Bertone→Saxe cairn 1 h up + 0.5 h down · balcony to Bonatti 2 h · descent to camp 1 h. Headtorch in the pack for the final stretch.

📍 Bailout = stay on the balcony. If you skip the Saxe cairn, Day 6 drops to ~22 km / +1,275 m / 7–8 h and gives you a relaxed Bonatti sunset. The Saxe out-and-back adds ~+350 m / 1.5 h.

Note on the map: the GPX polyline shows the bail-to-balcony version — the Saxe cairn detour isn't drawn on the map but is a clearly signed side-trip from Bertone.

You could also drop the pack at the Bertone junction, hike up to the cairn light, then collect it on the way back — saves your back on the steep bit.

🦶 Route

From Champex, walk to Relais d’Arpette, then enter the wild Val d’Arpette. Climb through boulder fields and scree to the Fenêtre d’Arpette (2,665m) — a narrow rock window with the Trient Glacier filling the view. Steep, loose descent to Trient.

⛺ Where to Sleep

Camping Le Peuty / Refuge Le Peuty (1,328m) — basic but atmospheric. Toilets and water. Alternative: Hôtel Col de la Forclaz.

  • Lunch: carry! No food between Arpette and Trient
  • Dinner: eat at Refuge Le Peuty (~CHF 25) or Hôtel Col de la Forclaz

💡 Tips

Start early. In bad weather, take the Bovine route instead (easier, scenic Rhône valley views). Upper section is steep scree — trekking poles essential.

From Trient, climb through forest to Col de Balme (2,191m) — the Swiss-French border. The 360° panorama here is extraordinary: Mont Blanc massif on one side, Swiss Valais on the other. This is the classic TMB “postcard view.” Descend into France via the high path to Tré-le-Champ.

Bivouac near Tré-le-Champ — you’re back in France. Stay below the Aiguilles Rouges reserve boundary (signed) to avoid needing a reservation. Or book a spot at Lacs des Cheserys via reserve-bivouac74.fr if you want to push higher into the reserve.

Camping rule: Aiguilles Rouges reserve — bivouac ONLY in the designated Plan du Brévent zone. ✅ Reserved via reserve-bivouac74.fr. Pitch after 7 PM, strike camp by 9 AM, leave no trace.
📏Distance7.3

⬇Descent125m
⏱Time3–5h

🦶 Route

Pre-dawn start from Cheserys — hike up to Lac Blanc (30–40 min) for sunrise over Mont Blanc. Continue to La Flégère (1,877m). Follow the Grand Balcon Sud towards Le Brévent — this section includes the famous échelles (metal ladders bolted into rock faces) and chain-assisted passages. 4–5 short ladder sections with some exposure — well-secured but dramatic. Continue via Planpraz to Le Brévent (2,525m). Drop just below the summit to the designated Plan du Brévent bivouac zone in the Aiguilles Rouges reserve and pitch the tent after 7 PM. Sunset over Mont Blanc, sunrise over the Aiguilles — last night on the trail, possibly the best view of the trip.

⛺ Where to Sleep

Bivouac at Plan du Brévent (2,405m) — designated zone in the Aiguilles Rouges Nature Reserve. ✅ Reserved via reserve-bivouac74.fr. Pitch after 7 PM, strike camp by 9 AM. No water on site — fill up at Refuge du Brévent or La Flégère earlier in the day.

🎒 Carry
Breakfast (or eat at Lac Blanc), final dinner + tomorrow's breakfast, snacks, water!
🏠 Eat at refuge/restaurant
Lunch at Refuge du Lac Blanc or Flégère, optional dinner at Refuge du Brévent before the bivouac
🛒 Resupply
None — finish what you brought from Champex

🍽 Food & Resupply Details

  • Breakfast: cook at Cheserys before dawn, or eat at Refuge du Lac Blanc after sunrise
  • Lunch: Refuge La Flégère or Le Brévent summit restaurant
  • Dinner: cook at the Plan du Brévent bivouac (last camp dinner of the trip!) or eat at Refuge du Brévent just before pitching
  • Fill all water bottles at La Flégère or Refuge du Brévent — there is no water source at the bivouac zone

💡 Tips

Start before dawn at Cheserys — 30–40 min to Lac Blanc for sunrise. 🪨 The échelles between La Flégère and Brévent are fine in dry conditions but slippery when wet — check the forecast. If uncomfortable with exposure, take the La Flégère cable car to Planpraz and rejoin from there. Note that today is short on paper (7 km, ~620 m up) — the point is to arrive at Brévent unhurried, take pictures, eat at the refuge, then drop down to the bivouac after 7 PM for one final magical night under the Mont Blanc face. The descent to Les Houches comes tomorrow morning.

Timeline: Start 6:00 AM → Lac Blanc sunrise 6:30 → La Flégère 9:00 → Brévent 12:00 → lazy afternoon at the refuge → pitch tent 19:00 at Plan du Brévent.

💡 Tips

The descent from Brévent to Les Houches is long but mostly on forest path — knee-friendly with poles. If legs are toast, take the Brévent cable car down to Chamonix instead and bus to Les Houches (or skip Les Houches entirely and grab the FlixBus from Chamonix). Aim for the 14:00 FlixBus to leave buffer; the 16:00 cuts it close if there's traffic. Shower opportunity: GVA airport has paid showers near the gates if you want to be clean for the flight.
